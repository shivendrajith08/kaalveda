## A Journey Older Than History

Tourism in Egypt is itself astonishingly old. Greek and Roman travellers were already sightseeing at the pyramids two thousand years ago, scratching graffiti onto monuments that were ancient even then. Today, heritage tourism is one of the pillars of the [modern Egyptian](/article/modern-egypt) economy and one of the great bucket-list journeys on Earth.

## The Essential Route

A classic visit traces the Nile from north to south:

1. **Cairo & Giza** — the [Pyramids of Giza](/article/pyramids-of-giza) and the Great Sphinx, plus the new **Grand Egyptian Museum**, the largest archaeological museum in the world, holding the full treasure of Tutankhamun.
2. **Luxor** — ancient Thebes, with the colossal temples of **Karnak** and **Luxor** on the east bank and the **Valley of the Kings** and **Valley of the Queens** on the west.
3. **Aswan & Abu Simbel** — the southern frontier, where the rock-cut temples of **Ramesses II** were famously relocated, block by block, to save them from the rising waters of the Aswan dam.
4. **The Nile cruise** — many travellers sail between Luxor and Aswan, seeing the riverside temples of Edfu and Kom Ombo along the way.

:::insight Saving the monuments
The 1960s rescue of Abu Simbel — cutting the temples into blocks and rebuilding them on higher ground — sparked the international movement that created the **UNESCO World Heritage** system. Egypt's monuments quite literally helped invent the modern idea of shared human heritage.
:::

## Travel as Understanding

Standing before a wall of [hieroglyphs](/article/hieroglyphs) or inside a royal tomb does something no book can: it collapses the distance of millennia. The traveller becomes the final step on the long journey we have followed — from the founding of [ancient Egypt](/article/ancient-egypt), through its gods, kings, and monuments, all the way back to the living, breathing river today.

> Every visitor who climbs the Giza plateau joins a line of travellers stretching back, unbroken, for more than two thousand years.
