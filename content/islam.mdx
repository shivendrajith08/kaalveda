## A Word Meaning Surrender

The Arabic word *islam* means **submission** — the willing surrender of the whole self to the will of the one God, **Allah**, a name that means simply "the God." One who makes that surrender is a *muslim*, "one who submits." At the centre of the faith stands a single sentence, the **shahada**: *There is no god but God, and Muhammad is the messenger of God.* To say these words sincerely is to become a Muslim. Everything else in the religion flows from that stark and uncompromising declaration of the oneness of God.

That oneness — *tawhid* — is the beating heart of Islam. God is utterly singular, without partner, without offspring, without image. He is the creator of all that is, the merciful and the just, nearer to a person, the [Quran](/article/the-quran) says, than their own jugular vein. To associate anything else with Him — to worship an idol, to divide Him into persons, to make any creature His equal — is *shirk*, the one sin the tradition holds beyond easy forgiveness. Where [Christianity](/article/christianity) confesses one God in three persons and [Hinduism](/article/hinduism) sees the divine in a thousand forms, Islam insists on an absolute, undivided unity.

:::insight The youngest faith, the oldest claim
Islam does not present itself as a new religion at all, but as the final restoration of the oldest one. It teaches that God sent the same message through a long line of prophets — Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus and many more — and that humanity repeatedly forgot or corrupted it. Muhammad is held to be the *seal of the prophets*, the last in that line, through whom the message was given a final, perfect and unalterable form. To a Muslim, Abraham was a Muslim, and so was Jesus: not founders of rival faiths but earlier messengers of the same eternal truth.
:::

## The Book and the Prophet

Islam rests on two foundations. The first is the [Quran](/article/the-quran), which Muslims hold to be the literal, uncreated speech of God, revealed to [Muhammad](/article/muhammad) through the angel Gabriel over twenty-three years and preserved without change ever since. It is not a book *about* God in the way the [Bible](/article/the-bible) is a library of human testimony; it is understood as God's own words, which is why it is recited in the original Arabic the world over and why its beauty of sound is itself considered a miracle.

The second foundation is the example of the Prophet himself — his sayings and deeds, gathered as the **hadith** and woven together into the **sunna**, the path of the Prophet. Where the Quran is silent or brief, the remembered life of Muhammad guides the believer. From the Quran and the sunna the scholars of Islam built the **sharia**, a comprehensive way of life that reaches from prayer and pilgrimage to commerce, marriage and the conduct of the state.

> God: there is no god but Him, the Ever Living, the Ever Watchful. Neither slumber nor sleep overtakes Him. All that is in the heavens and the earth belongs to Him.

That passage, the famous "Throne Verse," captures the Quranic vision of a God boundless and sovereign over all creation — the God to whom the Muslim surrenders.

## A Religion You Practise

Islam is less a set of doctrines to be believed than a life to be lived, and its daily shape is given by the [Five Pillars](/article/five-pillars) — the testimony of faith, the five daily prayers, the giving of alms, the fast of Ramadan, and the pilgrimage to Mecca. These are not optional devotions but the very frame of a Muslim life, binding the believer five times a day to the rhythm of prayer and once a year, if they are able, into the vast white-robed throng circling the Kaaba.

:::note One faith, many houses
Like every great tradition, Islam is not a single monolith. Its largest division, between [Sunni and Shia](/article/sunni-shia-divide), opened within a generation of the Prophet's death over the question of who should lead the community. Alongside them runs the mystical current of **Sufism**, which seeks not law alone but the direct experience of God's love, echoing the contemplative paths of [Christian mystics](/article/christian-mysticism) and the yogis of [India](/article/hinduism). From the deserts of Arabia to the rice fields of Indonesia, Islam has taken on the colours of every culture it touched while holding fast to the one creed at its core.
:::

## The Civilization It Built

Within a hundred years of Muhammad's death in 632, Arab armies carrying the new faith had overthrown the Persian Empire, stripped the Eastern Roman Empire of its richest provinces, and ruled an arc of territory larger than Rome at its height. But the deeper conquest was of the mind. While much of Christian Europe forgot the learning of antiquity after the [fall of Rome](/article/fall-of-rome), the scholars of the [Islamic Golden Age](/article/islamic-golden-age) gathered, translated and surpassed the science and philosophy of [Greece](/article/greek-philosophy), India and Persia, giving the world algebra, advances in medicine and astronomy, and the very numerals these words are written beside.
