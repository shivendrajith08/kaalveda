## A Wonder That Outlasted the Rest

Of the **Seven Wonders of the Ancient World**, only one still stands: the **Great Pyramid of Khufu** at Giza. Completed around 2560 BCE, it held the record as the tallest structure made by human hands for nearly **3,800 years**, until the cathedral spires of medieval Europe finally surpassed it. Beside it rise the pyramids of **Khafre** (which looks taller because it sits on higher ground and still keeps a cap of its original casing) and the smaller **Menkaure**, guarded by the **Great Sphinx**.

## Precision Without Iron

What stuns engineers is not just the size but the **accuracy**. The Great Pyramid's square base, some 230 metres on a side, is level to within a couple of centimetres, and its faces are aligned to true north with an error of only a fraction of a degree — achieved without iron tools, the compass, or modern surveying instruments.

- An estimated **2.3 million blocks**, averaging over two tonnes each.
- Original height **146.6 m**; a smooth casing of fine white limestone, now largely stripped away.
- Internal chambers, relieving stones, and the soaring **Grand Gallery**, all cut and fitted with extraordinary care.

:::insight Not built by slaves
The old image of whip-driven slaves is a myth. Archaeology at the workers' village shows a paid, fed, and housed workforce of skilled craftsmen and rotating labourers — organised, provisioned, and even given medical care by the Egyptian state.
:::

## Tombs Aligned to the Sky

The pyramids were **royal tombs**, designed to launch the dead king into the heavens and eternal life. Their orientation was no accident: the Egyptians used the stars to fix true north, tying their architecture to the rhythms of the sky — a connection explored in [Egyptian astronomy](/article/egyptian-astronomy). Causeways linked each pyramid to a valley temple by the Nile, framing the whole complex as a machine for resurrection.

> The shape itself may evoke the **benben** — the primeval mound that rose from the waters of creation, and the petrified rays of the sun god descending to the earth.

The pyramids are the supreme statement of a wider tradition of sacred building that runs through Egyptian temples and on into the story of [ancient architecture](/article/ancient-architecture). Today they remain the single most visited monument in the country — the gateway to [Egyptian tourism](/article/egypt-tourism).
